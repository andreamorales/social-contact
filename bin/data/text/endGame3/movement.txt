This implied a lot of back and forth, re-thinking our frontiers at every step we took.
With it came significant migration periods, and population fluxes were a concern for those among us that were in charge.
I didn't sign up for all the moving back and forth; but I wasn't the only to be affected by the turmoil, so I kept quiet. And I moved, again and again.
We stood still. Throughout the ages, few things remained the same, but our borders were indeed a constant.
We saw other nations change shape as they changed course, and yet we decided to resist any incentive to spread out, or to shelter.
I will probably die in the same place I was born; and so will everybody else. Our roots are our identity.
